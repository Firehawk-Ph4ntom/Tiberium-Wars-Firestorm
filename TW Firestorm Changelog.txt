New Unit/Structure and Upgrade Additions:
- Added 3 new Mutant units to the Dropship Bay!:
  > "Salvaged Pitbull": Tier 1 Scout unit that is good vs. Light Vehicles and Aircraft. Has an Anti-Infantry/Anti-Structure Mortar, and can fire an EMP mortar round through an ability
  > "Salvaged Humvee": Tier 1 Anti-Infantry/Anti-Aircraft unit, has a repair drone, and can garrison an infantry unit inside it. It is a great support unit, and has a mini-rocket pod to help with light Vehicles
  > "Salvaged APC": Tier 1 Reckoner/Surveyor Vehicle Hybrid. It can deploy into a stationary bunker to accomodate more garrison slots, while also providing build radius and can detect stealth, and can repack back up to an APC
  > "Salvaged Devils Tongue": Tier 2 Anti-Infantry/Anti-Structure/Anti-Garrison unit, functions just like the Liquifier Tank without the Subterranean move ability, but has a garrison slot
  > "Salvaged Mammoth Tank": Tier 3 Heavily armored unit. Akin to the Mammoth Tank, but with a garrison slot. This unit, while weaker, is much cheaper to produce and has decent crowd control capabilities
  > "Salvaged MLRS": Tier 3 Heavy Artillery unit. The unit is just a standard artillery tank, but unlike all the other tanks that have GRENADE damage type and are good vs infantry and structures mostly, this one can kill vehicles much quicker, and has a decent size AOE
(The only way to unlock and use these units for now is capturing the Dropship Bay with a Mutant Salvager)

- Added a new upgrade to the "Tiberium Chemical Plant": "Liquified Contamination Mix", which, just like "Corrosive Ichor Mix" upgrade for Scrin, "Tiberium Troopers" and "Liquifier Tanks" now spawn Contamination Zones with their weapon, and can be further upgraded with "Blue Tiberium Core"

- Added a new upgrade to the "Stasis Chamber": "Overlord's Calling", which increases the squad size of Shock Troopers, Ravagers, and Seismic Troopers, improving their combat effectiveness

- Renamed "The Ascended" to "Cyborg Pummelers" and replaced its model with a modified "Enlightened" model from KW
  > Added the "EMP Blast" ability to the unit

- Added new Air Transports for both GDI and Nod Factions!:
  > Condor Carryall: This Air Transport is for GDI, and unlike the V35-Ox, this transport is more durable, but moves much slower
  > Nightwind Transport: Nod's new Air Transport, which unlike the Carryall, it moves superbly fast, but isn't as tanky as the Carryall

- Wolverines can now destroy missile projectiles on close range!

- Added new voice lines and sound effects to Tiberian Fiends!

- Added new variations of Crates!:
  > Firepower Crate: Units collecting this Crate will receive 25% extra attack damage
  > Armor Crate: Units collecting this Crate will receive 25% extra armor
  > Mobility Crate: Units collecting this Crate will receive 25% extra movement speed
  > Rate-Of-Fire Crate: Units collecting this Crate will receive 25% extra Fire Rate
  > Attack Range Crate: Units collecting this Crate will receive 25% extra attack and vision range
  > Cloaking Crate: Units collecting this Crate will become stealthed!
  > Tiberium Field Crate: Units collding with this Crate will spawn a miniature Tiberium Field
(For Each unit affected by power-up crates, a special icon will display over that specific unit as well)

Skirmish AI Changes:

######################

Due to the reworks that happened to the PVE Gamemode option mentioned, the Skirmish AI has had to have undergone a few vital changes, mainly due to Desyncs in multiplayer, and the fact that the AI started to break. However, that means that the Personality list is now utterly cluttered with duplicates of each personality, and that 'Random' personality doesn't filter out the duplicates from each other.

Essentially, when playing with PVE Gamemode off, the AI works as intended, and no issues happen there. However, when PVE Gamemode is toggled on, the AI would stop building properly, would stop capturing Neutral/Enemy structures, Scrin AI will stop building their "Mastermind" unit, and in multiplayer, the game would desync frequently. As a result, and the only so far I've found to fix this issue, was either revert the changes done to the PVE Gamemode and revert all the bug fixes and glitches, and re-introduce all the bugs back as well as the UI inconsistencies. The codebase also became utterly messy before when this gamemode prototype was first made, and I'd have to re-mess up my codebase xD

Or, the second option: Since we can't actually add in new AI difficulty options, then new personalities had to exist that were dedicated to the gamemode. Though, this also introduced the issue where the wrong AI personality for the wrong gamemode type would break the game again, and that 'Random' personality is no longer safe in multiplayer (MP desyncs from wrong personality chosen). But this is the limit of what I can do for now.

In the future, I think the best way forward would be to gut one of the gamemodes, but we shall see
